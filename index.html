    • DANCE TEACHERS

      Nektarios Foris

      Nektarios was born in Nicosia and is the owner of the dance academy. He soon became interested in the Cypriot and Greek dance and music as he was greatly influenced by his close relative Gregori Assioti, who was the founder of the first dance group in Cyprus in 1957. At the age of six he started taking Cypriot and Greek traditional dance lessons. As a member of folkloric groups he performed in events in Cyprus and in festivals abroad and he made appearances in many television shows as well (see below for details). He has been teaching Cypriot folkloric dances (Antikristoi, Sousta, Sirtos, Stamna, Drepani and Tatsia) and Greek dances such as Chasapiko, Sousta, Sirtaki, Kalamatiano, Aptaliko, Macedonian, Ikariotiko, Sirto,
      Nisiotiko, Pentozalis, Tsamiko, Tsifteteli, Zeimpekiko to students from six until eighty years old! He also teaches Latin dances
      like Cha-Cha, Jive, Roumba, Samba, Salsa, Bachata,
      salsa Shine, Merenque, Paso Doble and Tango.


    • Savvas Savva

      Savvas was born in Nicosia, Cyprus and became avidly interested in the art of dance and movement at a very young age. He participated in traditional performances at school and began to take advanced private lessons where he expanded his knowledge on traditional culture and technique with regards to Cypriot and Greek dance. As a teenager Savvas began to experiment and master his techniques in modern dance styles including hip-hop and MJ breakdance styles. He also became very interested in Latin American dances, an interest he continued to explore in depth as an adult.
      With over ten years of performing and teaching experience, he continuously evolved his personal style through bootcamps, seminars and workshops both in Cyprus and abroad, helping him solidly develop his personal style and tecnique. Today he is a sought after professional and continues to teach dancers of all ages and experience levels as well as perform on a group and individual basis in public arenas and on national media outlets.
      He has a passion for experimental and classic choreography and enjoys positioning himself as both a team player and a team leader in professional dance crews across the island.



    • Christiana Giap

      Christiana was born in Larnaca in 1997. She has had a passion for dance for as long back as she remembers. From the age of 4 until now, she has joined several dance schools both in Greece and Cyprus, where she was taught different types of dance such as Ballet, Tap dancing, Bollywood, Latin, Hip hop, Reggaeton, Dancehall and Traditional Greek and Cypriot dances.
      She joined Dance Academy in 2014 as an assistant teacher and now, she constitutes one of the main dance teachers. She teaches hip hop to kids of ages between 6 to 15 years old and starting this year, she will be teaching reggaeton to both kids and adults. Also, she is part of the professional team of the academy. Furthermore, she has participated in several Salsa congresses which took place in Cyprus and Slovenia. She has also recieved many diplomas from the International Dance Teachers Association (I.D.T.A).
      Christiana graduated from Apostolos Varnavas Lyceum in 2015 and currently, she is studying Computer Science at the University of Cyprus. Her dream is to continue dancing for the rest of her life.


    • Polina Carlettidou

      Polina was born in Nicosia in 1996. From a very young age she became interested in the art of dance and movement and started taking rhythmic gymnastics lessons. However, her first encounter with dance, was at primary school where she participated in traditional performances. Later in years, she became interested in various other dances and started taking Contemporary, Hip-Hop, Latin and Reggaeton classes. Despite her young age, she has already attended various dance seminars, performed in a number of TV shows and Salsa Congresses both in Cyprus and Slovenia and acquired many diplomas from the International Dance Teachers Association (I.D.T.A). In 2014, she became a member of our dance school and started teaching Latin American dances to children between 4 and 13 years old. She has also worked as an assistant teacher in adult Latin groups. She is also a member of our professional team as well as the belly dance competition team. She graduated from the GC School of Careers and decided to continue her studies in the United Kingdom. She is now studying Biomedical Sciences at the University of Surrey.
      At the same time, she decided that she wanted to expand her knowledge in Latin and Ballroom dance sport (Cha-cha, Jive, Samba, Rumba etc.)
